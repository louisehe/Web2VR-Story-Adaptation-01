AL KHOR, Qatar — The Americans held their own against England in their second group game of the World Cup on Friday, drawing 0-0 with one of the world’s best teams. The U.S. created more scoring opportunities, many of which were led by Weston McKennie on the right wing.

In the 25th minute, McKennie stood on the far right side of the field, briefly pausing with the ball at his feet while he took stock of his teammates’ positions.

He dribbled past two England defenders and then passed to Yunus Musah at the top of the box. (Keep your eye on McKennie’s movement off the ball.)

As Musah dribbled toward the end line, McKennie started to make a run into the box and toward the penalty spot.

McKennie lifted his arm to signal that he’d been left unmarked.

Meanwhile, the Americans patiently worked the ball back around to the top of the box. Tim Weah, wide open, took a big touch forward and lined up to deliver a cross into the middle.

Christian Pulisic darted toward the near post, dragging an English defender with him, leaving a pocket of open space for McKennie behind him.

As Weah’s cross floated in, McKennie was free in the box with half the goal gaping.

McKennie struck the ball on the half-volley, skyrocketing it over the goal and into the stands.

With two ties in the group phase, the Americans must beat Iran in their next game if they hope to advance to the knockout stage.
