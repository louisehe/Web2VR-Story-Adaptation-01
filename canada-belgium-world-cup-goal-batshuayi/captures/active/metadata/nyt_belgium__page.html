AL RAYYAN, Qatar — Belgium, the second-ranked team in the world and a tournament favorite, eked out a win over Canada in its opening match of the World Cup on Wednesday. The young, plucky Canadian team was faster and fresher, but in the end it hardly mattered: Michy Batshuayi’s 44th-minute goal proved decisive.

It started from Belgium’s back line. Toby Alderweireld had possession, but there were no midfield options — Canada’s attackers had dropped in, while its back line pushed forward — cutting off the Belgian midfield.

Alderweireld looked up, lobbed a perfectly weighted ball over the top and bypassed the entire Canadian team.

Batshuayi held his run, careful to stay onside, moving down the center of the field to meet the ball in flight.

Canada’s defenders raced to recover, but Batshuayi was one step ahead.

Batshuayi anticipated the flight of the ball, letting it bounce once before striking a half-volley into the net.

Belgium earned 3 points and now stands atop Group F, after Croatia and Morocco played to a scoreless draw on Wednesday.
